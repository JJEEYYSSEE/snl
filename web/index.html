🐍 How to Play Snakes & Lenders

×

🎯 The Core Objective

Be the first player to land exactly on tile 100. If your roll would overshoot 100, the move is invalid and you stay put.

💰 Economy & Income

Unlike classic luck-based games, you earn points by moving across tiles (deeper tiles give more points). You spend this precious currency in the Snake Shop to place tactical traps. Income is scarce, so timing and budget management are key!

🛒 The Snake Shop

During the start of your turn, if you are on the board, you can buy a snake:

  • Sub-linear Pricing: Shorter snakes are very cheap; long, devastating traps cost significantly more. Additionally, the price escalates with each snake you purchase!
  • Limit: You can own at most 3 active snakes on the board at once.
  • Placement Rules: Placements are allowed between head tiles 20 and 90. You cannot chain snakes head-to-tail or place on occupied/ladder tiles.
  • Anti-Wall: You cannot place a snake that would extend a consecutive run of adjacent snake heads past 2.

🐍 Strike Ranges & Immunity

  • Board snakes bite only on exact head landing.
  • Player snakes have an active **strike range** covering the head and 4 tiles below it.
  • Immunity: You are 100% immune to your own traps.
  • Point Theft: Getting bitten slides you back to the tail AND **robs** points from your wallet, transferring them directly to the trap's owner!
  • Once a player snake bites, it is **consumed** and removed from the board.

💣 Bombs & Bankruptcy

There are 5 fixed bomb tiles on the board. Landing on one inflicts a heavy depth-scaled point penalty. If your wallet goes below 0, you go **BANKRUPT**!

☠️ BANKRUPTCY RESETS YOU TO TILE 0 WITH ZERO POINTS! Use safety cushions!

🤖 AI Opponents

  • Easy (Expectimax): deliberate weak heuristic, reacts late, hoards points, only buys short cheap traps.
  • Hard (PPO Neural Network): trained via reinforcement learning self-play, places catch-optimal traps, parks win-denials near the finish line, manages points aggressively.
